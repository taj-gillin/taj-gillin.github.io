# ATA: Artificial Teaching Assistant

An intelligent teaching assistant that adapts to specific course environments to help students with conceptual, debugging, and logistical questions. Built for Hack@Brown 2024 and winner of the **Best Use of AI in Education by Schmidt Futures** award, now deployed across multiple Brown University courses.

**Team:** Taj Gillin, Julian Dai, Woody Hulse  
**Event:** Hack@Brown 2024  
**Award:** Best Use of AI in Education by Schmidt Futures

---

## The Challenge in Modern Education

As both students and teaching assistants in the Brown CS department, we experienced firsthand the critical issues facing modern education. Students struggle to receive the help they need to learn effectively, while TAs are overwhelmed trying to meet the needs of hundreds of students.

Our research revealed a stark reality: **79% of students** report using ChatGPT before going to office hours, and **50%** use it before even trying EdStem. Meanwhile, **two-thirds of TAs** report being asked the same questions repeatedly, creating an inefficient cycle that leaves everyone frustrated.

## A Course-Specific Solution

### The ATA Approach

Unlike generic AI tools, ATA (Artificial Teaching Assistant) is designed specifically for educational environments. It uses retrieval augmented generation (RAG) to access relevant course materials including syllabi, assignment specifications, code examples, and even previous EdStem discussions.

The key innovation is **course adaptation** - ATA doesn't just answer questions, it provides responses informed by the specific context of each course, ensuring students get help that aligns with their instructor's teaching goals and assignment requirements.

### Pedagogical Design

ATA is built with a pedagogical approach that encourages learning rather than just providing answers. Instead of simply giving solutions, it guides students through their thought process, asking them to explain their reasoning and helping them discover answers themselves.

This approach is reinforced through carefully crafted examples and chain-of-thought reasoning, ensuring that ATA responds in a way that promotes understanding and critical thinking.

### Maintaining Educational Integrity

ATA is designed to support learning, not replace it. When students ask for direct answers or solutions, the system is programmed to redirect them toward understanding the concepts and working through problems themselves.

## Technical Implementation

### Retrieval Augmented Generation

ATA uses RAG to provide context-aware responses by retrieving relevant snippets from course materials. This includes:
- Assignment specifications and requirements
- Course syllabi and learning objectives
- Code examples and solutions
- Previous EdStem discussions and Q&A

### AI-Powered Guidance

The system employs GPT-4 with few-shot learning techniques to ensure responses are both helpful and pedagogically sound. By training on carefully crafted examples, ATA learns to:
- Ask guiding questions rather than provide direct answers
- Encourage students to explain their thought process
- Provide hints and suggestions that lead to understanding
- Maintain a supportive, educational tone

### Safety and Content Filtering

Before displaying responses to students, ATA filters both queries and responses for malicious intent, ensuring that the AI provides appropriate, educational guidance without compromising academic standards.

The system's ability to adapt to different courses makes it valuable across various subjects and teaching styles. By pulling from course-specific materials, ATA ensures that its guidance aligns with each instructor's approach and learning objectives.

## Technical Challenges

### RAG Implementation
Implementing effective retrieval augmented generation required careful design of document chunking, embedding strategies, and similarity search to ensure relevant course materials are retrieved for each query.

### Pedagogical Response Design
Creating an AI that responds in a pedagogically sound way required extensive prompt engineering and few-shot learning to ensure responses encourage learning rather than just providing answers.

### Safety and Content Filtering
Ensuring that ATA maintains educational integrity required implementing robust filtering systems to prevent inappropriate responses and maintain academic standards.
